The Eiffel Tower: Parisian Icon and Symbol of France

Features

Unique Lattics Structure

The Eiffel Tower is renowned for its intricate iron lattice structure. Composed of over 18,000 individual iron parts and joined by 2.5 million rivets, this design was groundbreaking during its construction and remains a defining feature.

Iconic Height

One of the most striking features of the Eiffel Tower is its impressive height. Standing at 324 meters (1,063 feet), it held the title of the world's tallest man-made structure for over 40 years after its completion in 1889.

Illuminated Splendor

The Eiffel Tower is famous for its nighttime illumination. It features a mesmerizing light show with thousands of sparkling LED lights, creating a spectacular display that showcases its elegance and beauty.

About

  • Iconic Architecture: Designed by Gustave Eiffel, the Eiffel Tower was constructed as the centerpiece of the 1889 Exposition Universelle (World's Fair) held in Paris to celebrate the 100th anniversary of the French Revolution
  • Symbol of French Ingenuity: The Eiffel Tower is a testament to French engineering and artistic prowess. Its innovative design and impressive height (324 meters or 1,063 feet) made it the tallest man-made structure in the world at the time of its completion.
  • Romantic Icon: The Eiffel Tower's romantic ambiance has made it a popular spot for couples and proposals. Its charm is amplified by the nearby Seine River and picturesque surroundings, making it an ideal location for romance.
